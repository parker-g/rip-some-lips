here's a collection of knots you'll surely not ever forget!

clinch knot

  1. First, guide your line through the eye of the lure. Be sure to pull 3-5 inches of line through.
  2. Now, begin wrapping that excess line around the main line. BE SURE to leave a hole of space between the eye of the lure and your first wrap. Around six wraps should do the trick.
  3. After completing the amount of wraps you desire, take the end of your line and feed it through the hole you left in step two.
  4. Now, pull your line tight.

improved clinch knot

the improved clinch is almost identical to a clinch knot, only differing in the last step.

  1. Begin by guiding your line through the eye, creating 3-5 inches of excess.
  2. Next, begin wrapping the excess around the main line. Again, BE SURE to leave a hole of space between the eye of the lure and your first wrap.
  3. After completing six to eight wraps around the main line, feed the tip of your line into the hole you left in the previous step.
  4. Now, feed the tip of your line through the pocket you just created.
  5. Finally, you can pull your line tight. Not too bad right?

palomar knot

palomar knot is strong and simple.

  1. Begin by doubling up your line, folding it in on itself to create around 6 inches of doubled line.
  2. Now, feed the doubled line through the eye of the lure.
  3. Perform an overhand knot, better known as a loop de loop, with the doubled line.
  4. Finally, pass the lure through the head of the doubled line.
  5. Now pull 'er tight!
